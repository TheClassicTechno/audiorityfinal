THE CHALLENGES

The hearing impaired are at a significantly higher risk than others in outdoor environments, as they cannot hear background noises, such as gunshots firing or a car honking as clearly as non-hearing impaired people do. According to researchers from JAMA Otolaryngology, people who have trouble hearing are 90% more likely to be injured outside their homes.

Many hearing impaired members cannot afford high-quality hearing aids, as most conventional hearing aids cost thousands of dollars.

Besides being expensive, hearing aids are also specifically made to identify human speech only and is not intended to detect non-speech audio in one’s surroundings. In addition, hearing aids amplify both background noise and human speech, making it difficult to clearly hear them separately.

Currently, there are not many robust technical solutions out there that detect non-word background noises specifically. As for machine learning (ML), its technology is still quite new and ML solutions are not widely in use yet in many sectors.

There are not many extensive, accessible, and free audio datasets to use to train machine learning solutions to detect audio.

Many existing audio classification machine learning algorithms are inaccurate, too complex , and/or large to deploy as a real-world solution for the hearing impaired in places they’re needed.

MY INSPIRATION & PERSONAL STORY

My grandma became hearing impaired 5 years ago.

  • She currently lives in a bustling town in a foreign country. The best hearing aids she could buy with the little money she has are not very effective, as our family members still need to raise our voices to talk to her and walk with her when she is going outside. However, there are always times when no family member is available to take her outside and not every hearing impaired member has someone else to take care of them.

There needs to be an accessible, affordable, AND accurate solution.

  • We need to build a more convenient and low-cost solution that does not sacrifice accuracy for hearing impaired members, especially members who live in low-income communities.

THE OBJECTIVE

Guiding Question

  • Is there a more accurate, affordable, accessible, and robust mechanism that can identify and classify outdoor environmental sounds?

The Task

  • Find a large, publicly available dataset that contain labeled mono audios for a wide range of environmental sounds
  • Create, edit, optimize, and train a deep learning model to extract important features of audio to identify and classify them accurately.
  • Create a mobile app and deploy the machije learning model into the app for testing
